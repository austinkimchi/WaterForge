Week 3

We had a total of two meetings, one with Rebecaa (Forge Garden Partner) and one for our group.

In the first meeting with Rebecca, we asked her our interview questions which can be found in the Partner Interview supplements.

In our meeting, we discussed our assigned homework which was to research the required equipment, and viability in terms of requirements, and cost.


Week 4

We conducted our first minute meeting on Oct 16th, 2024, discussing the visit to Forge Garden.

Tiffany, one of our team members, went to the Forge Garden on Oct 15th, 2024 (Thursday), to discuss with Rebecca about our idea of implementing the primary plan (found in research slides).


Week 5

This week I have completed my flow calculations, and I discovered that the flow velocity at spout is 3.35 m/s with with 410 GPH and our conditions. View supplement of flow calculation for more information.

I have also contributed to my teams design choices by researching different components, weighing their benefits, in order to get an order ready once we recieve a reponse from the tribe.

Due to not recieving a timely reponse from the Muwekma Ohlone Tribe, we did not host a minute meeting this week, as it would not be productive.


Week 6

We could not conduct a minute this week as we are still waiting for a response from the Muwekma Ohlone Tribe.

We have sent a follow up email to try to speed up the operation. We have also recieved information that our partner leader was sick the previous week.

Our group has been working on a presentation to present for in class for the past week along with preparing a list of materials and prices to purchase.

In the following week, we plan to test compile the material lists and get design approval.


Week 7

Our team worked on the presentation and followed up with our community partner to recieve hastier responses.

This week I made more flow calculations and showed my derivation to ensure that I was not making any mistakes. I also computed the output velocity of the spout, in order for us to know which pump we would need for different pipe diameter sizes.

We are activley communicating with our partner to get our orders in and planned to purchase all materials by end of this week.

In week 8, we plan to test the flow of water through the grates while completing purchasing our materials.


Week 8

This week we purchased materials for our project including a copper pipe, solar panel with pump, abalone shells, rubber sealant, and JB weld.

Our current running total is near $145.00, over the $100.00 budget we had in mind.

A key decision we made was to move on without the tribe's response.

My team members created a new grate design that interlock because the 3D printer's size is too small to fit.

The following week, we plan to test the flow of water through the new grates while assembling our water feature in order to see if our design works.
